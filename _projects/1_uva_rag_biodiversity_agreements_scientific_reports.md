This UvA challenge focuses on a retrieval-augmented workspace for navigating international biodiversity agreements and the scientific reports that interpret, operationalize, or critique them, starting from the IUCN resolutions corpus.

### Typical fit

- source-grounded question answering over IUCN resolutions and related biodiversity-policy material
- comparison of international commitments, targets, and reporting logic
- linking scientific assessments to governance language and implementation questions
- local RAG prototyping that keeps the evidence path visible

### Why this matters

Practitioners and students often need to move between IUCN resolutions, CBD, IPBES, EEA, EUNIS, and related scientific or policy sources without collapsing them into vague summaries. This use case focuses on careful retrieval, visible source boundaries, auditable synthesis, and local deployment discipline rather than generic AI fluency.
