This use case turns The Hague's public monitoring and city-nature surfaces into one inspectable evidence environment for municipal biodiversity interpretation and communication.

### Typical fit

- navigating Groenmonitor, stadsnatuur themes, dashboards, StoryMaps, and map apps
- connecting municipal monitoring outputs to governance and council-facing context
- structuring reporting and interpretation across city trees, neighbourhood green, and city-nature themes
- packaging an evidence spine that remains traceable down to public source surfaces

### Why this matters

The Hague route shows how NatureDesk can work with a real municipal monitoring architecture instead of abstract examples. It is especially useful for demonstrating how public biodiversity evidence, thematic applications, and governance context can be turned into a coherent, source-linked story.
